How to Play

Race to empty your hand — but don't win a trick!

Goal

Get rid of all your cards without winning tricks. Winning a trick is bad — you take every played card into your hand. The last player still holding cards loses.

Setup

  • Standard 52-card deck, dealt evenly among players (2–8).
  • The player with the Ace of Spades leads first and must play it.

Opening Trick

  • Every player must follow spades if they have any.
  • If everyone follows → all played cards are discarded.
  • If anyone cuts (plays a non-spade), the trick ends immediately and the Ace of Spades holder takes all cards played so far.

Normal Tricks

  • Follow the led suit if possible; otherwise cut (play any card).
  • As soon as any player cuts, the trick ends — remaining players skip.
  • If all followed → all cards discarded.
  • If someone cut → highest card of the led suit wins and takes every played card into hand.

Who Leads Next

The player who played the highest card of the led suit leads the next trick — even when the trick was discarded.

Winning & Losing

  • Play your last card without winning the trick → you're out (a winner), ranked by finish order.
  • Last player holding cards is the loser.

Strategy Tips

  • When leading, burn high cards — they're most likely to win tricks later.
  • When following, play your highest card below the current winner to dump it safely.
  • Keep low cards of each suit so you can follow safely late in the game.
